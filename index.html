About this Visualization

Narrative visualization structure

This visualization is using interactive slideshow hybrid structure. Each slide contains some information and/or chart. On certain slide, the viewer can also dive deep to find out more details regarding those charts. It is best for the users to follow the slideshow but users are free to jump around the scenes.

Scenes

Each slide represents a scene. Some text only and some with both text and chart.

Annotations

The scenes (slides) have been annotated whenever necessary. For example, each chart has a short summary; each chart contains a title; most of the charts contain x and y axis labels. Data are labeled with legend and represented by color, size, shapes etc. When users hover around certain data points, more detailed information will appear in tooltips.

Parameters

Each chart has its own parameters, such as dataset, scales of axis, size of data points, etc. Certain slides contain options to change some parameters (dataset and results in different representations of data).

Triggers

The navigation buttons on top of the screen serve as triggers for scene change. Mouse click on those will enable users to navigate this visualization. Because each chart has its own parameters, scene change will also result in parameter changes. On all charts, hover (mouseover) can trigger tooltip display. On some charts, a drop down menu can trigger dataset and chart change.

Chronic Disease Mortality

Chronic diseases represented seven of the top 10 causes of death in the United States in 2010. These top 7 chronic disease contributed to about 2/3 of death in this US in 2010. It is important to study the mortality and risk factors over time to help federal and state agencies to plan and implement effective interventions. This visualization presents introductory, aggregated and trend data during 2010 and 2014 at the state and national level.

Questions to address

  1. What chronic disease caused most death during the period studied?
  2. What's the trend of the chronic disease mortality during the period studied?

What's showing here?

The overall picture of chronic disease mortality. The scatter plot shows the mortality against population at the state level from 2010 to 2014 for the selected 7 chronic disease (represented by 7 colored circles). Size of circles is proportional to mortality.

What can you explore?

  • The mortality difference among diseases.
  • The correlation of mortality in each state with its population.
  • The overall picture of what disease caused most death.
  • The actual data (absolute mortality) of each disease in state at certain year by hovering on data point (circle). The tooltip shows year, state and mortality for each data point.

What's showing here?

The total mortality of studied 7 chronic disease at the national level from 2010 to 2014.

What can you explore?

  • The top killer of the 7 chronic disease
  • The actual data (absolute mortality) of each disease at the national level during those 5 years studied by hovering on data point (bar).

What's showing here?

The trend of mortality (absolute or rate) studied 7 chronic disease at the national level from 2010 to 2014.

What can you explore?

  • The top killer of the 7 chronic disease
  • The trend of mortality (absolute) during those years. Please keep in mind that, there is only average data for cancer from 2010 to 2014. The yearly data for cancer were populated from the average data. In other words, the absolute mortality data for cancer from 2010 to 2014 are same.
  • The trend of mortality (rate) during those years (since US population was growing for those years, it is best to compare rate (absolute mortality/population)). This can be done by choose corresponding type of data from drop down menu.
  • The actual data (both absolute mortality and rate) of each disease at the national level during those 5 years studied by hovering on data point (circle).
